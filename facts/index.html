Facts

Regions

  1. Tidewater: The tidewater is the region of North Carolina located along the coast of North Carolina. All of the state's beaches are in the tidewater region.
  2. Coastal Plain: The coastal plain is home to some of the state's best farmland. Nestled between the urban cities and the coastal towns, the coastal plain is home to some of the state's most iconic food, from southern kitchen to outdoor barbecue.
  3. Piedmont: The Piedmont is located between the Coastal Plain and the mountains. The piedmont is a plateau, and is high and mostly flat. The piedmont is home to many of the state's cities, including Charlotte, Statesville, Raleigh, Durham, Chapel Hill, Fayetteville, Greensboro, and more.
  4. Mountains: The western part of the state is a section of the Appalachian mountains, including the Blue Ridge Mountains and the Great Smoky Mountains.

Features

Political Features

  • Charlotte, North Carolina has a population of approximately 859,000, making it the largest city in North Carolina.
  • North Carolina is made up of 13 congressional districts (view the maps page to learn more).

Physical Features

  • The Mountains of North Carolina are part of the second-largest mountain range in the US, which reaches from Alabama to Maine and Nova Scotia/Newfoundland
  • The coast of North Carolina is composed of hundreds of barrier islands, including Cape Hatteras and Cape Fear.
