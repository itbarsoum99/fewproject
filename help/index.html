How to Help

General

The best way to help the LGBTQ+ community is by being an ally. What does that mean? It means standing up for people when discrimination is occuring. It means stopping the comments when a person uses 'gay' as an insult or in a negative context. It means helping people up, both literally and figuratively, and standing by their side. It means being a friend, not out of obligation, but because you want to, without discrimination. It means accepting.

Reducing Discrimination

You can reduce discrimination targeting the LGBTQ+ community by objecting to discriminatory comments and standing up for people if and when they are harassed for their sexual orientation or gender identity. If you hear someone using 'gay' in a negative context (i.e. 'that's so gay'), explain to them that it's wrong to use 'gay' in a place where 'gross' or 'weird' is more appropriate, and that 'gay' does not equal weird. In another case, if you hear a trans person being misgendered or misnamed (i.e. a trans man being called by she/her pronouns), openly correct the person, and if they do it again, condemn them.

Advocacy for Justice

Advocate for the LGBTQ+ community. Take it beyond just the 'reducing discrimination' step, and start projects like FEW. Create groups of people that stand up for LGBTQ+ people. Educate people on what it means to be LGBTQ+. Respect and stand up for everyone in the LGBTQ+ community. Make sure that LGBTQ+ people have a voice in the greater community, and address inequalities with the people who have the authority to make change. Be the change you wish to see in the world (Gandhi).
